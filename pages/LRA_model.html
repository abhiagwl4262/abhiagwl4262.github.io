Low rank approximation of CNNs

  • by:Abhishek Agarwal

There is always talks about distilling of informtation from a big neural network into a small neural network. Speeding up the training and reducing the memory footprint is not only an interest for big tech companies who want to run AI as mobile services but also useful for practitioners with low resources.

CP Decomposition lays great foundation for low rank approximation of ND tensor. Canonical polyadic decomposition (CPD) may be regarded as a generalization of the matrix singular value decomposition (SVD) to tensors. Its very tricky to choose whether you want to decompose each 3D trained kernel seperately or the whole 4D weight tensor at Once. Deciding rank for Heigher dimension tensors is NP hard problem. tensorly is a great library when working with tensors and it provides off the shelf functions for tensor decomposition. In this library CP decomposition optimizes Frobenius norm of difference of constructed tensor and the original tensor using ALS (Alternating Least Squares).
